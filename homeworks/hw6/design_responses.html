    With regards to animation duration, the size of the screen can be crucial to the length of the animation. A smaller screen only needs a short time for objects to move a certain distance, while larger screens necessitate longer times for the same proportional distance. Other things to take into account are the size of the object(s) being transformed, and the overall complexity of the animation. There are many considerations to take into account with the transformation of objects, but it's ultimately about how you want to direct the user's eye, how to create the most natural-looking movement, and how to give the user the tools to anticipate the new state of the design.
